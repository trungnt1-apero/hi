# English Learning App — Level Framework

> **Standard**: CEFR | **Last updated**: 2026-05

## Overview

| Level | CEFR | Label | Target learner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Elementary | A1–A2 | Beginner | Young learners, first contact with English |
| B1 | B1 | Intermediate | Can handle everyday situations, familiar topics |
| B2 | B2 | Upper-Intermediate | Fluent with native speakers, complex texts |
| C1 | C1 | Advanced | Academic / professional fluency, implicit meaning |

## Full Framework

CAN ask someone using the key structures (about how they are and ask simple questions about belongings and preferences). CAN understand classroom instructions and respond with whole-body actions. CAN retell a story with picture support. | CAN read and understand words, including high-frequency words. CAN read and understand some simple sentences, including questions. CAN read and understand some short, simple stories about familiar topics with the help of pictures. CAN write short, simple phrases and sentences about pictures and familiar topics. CAN write short sentences about likes and dislikes. |
| **B1** — Intermediate | Understand the main points of clear standard input on familiar topics. Handle most everyday situations likely to arise while travelling. Produce simple connected text on familiar topics and personal interests. Describe experiences, events, and briefly give reasons for opinions and plans. | CAN follow the main points of extended discussions on familiar topics. CAN deal with most situations likely to arise when travelling in an English-speaking area. CAN express personal opinions and give straightforward reasons for them. CAN narrate a story or describe a personal experience using connected sentences. CAN understand the main idea of podcasts and videos on topics of personal interest. CAN write an email or message describing events, feelings and wishes. CAN keep a conversation going on a range of familiar topics using paraphrasing. CAN give a short, prepared talk on topics of personal interest. CAN follow the essential points in news reports on familiar events. | CAN follow the main points of extended discussions on familiar topics. CAN understand straightforward factual information about products or services. CAN keep a conversation going on a range of familiar topics using paraphrasing. CAN give a short, prepared talk on topics of personal interest. CAN narrate experiences and explain reasons clearly in everyday conversation. CAN follow the essential points in news reports on familiar events. | CAN read straightforward factual texts and understand the main information. CAN understand descriptions of events, feelings and wishes in personal letters. CAN write simple connected text on familiar topics and personal experiences. CAN write personal emails describing events, feelings and wishes. CAN summarise short passages of news articles in own words. |
| **B2** — Upper-Intermediate | Understand complex texts on concrete and abstract topics. Interact with native speakers with a degree of fluency and spontaneity. Produce clear, detailed text on a wide range of subjects and explain viewpoints. Develop arguments with clear advantages and disadvantages of various options. | CAN understand the main ideas in complex text including technical discussions in own field. CAN interact fluently and spontaneously with native speakers without strain for either party. CAN produce detailed, well-structured essays or reports on a broad range of subjects. CAN express and defend a viewpoint in a discussion by giving advantages and disadvantages. CAN follow extended academic or professional lectures and discussions. CAN understand most TV news, documentaries and movies without subtitles. CAN negotiate, making concrete proposals, handling objections and compromising. | CAN understand extended speech and lectures even when structure is not always clear. CAN follow most TV news and current affairs programmes. CAN take part effectively in discussions with native speakers on familiar topics. CAN present a clear, prepared talk with a viewpoint and support arguments with reasoning. CAN give and respond to complex hypothetical situations in conversations. CAN negotiate, making concrete proposals, handling objections and compromising. | CAN read articles and reports on contemporary problems with complex content. CAN understand contemporary literary prose and popular non-fiction. CAN write detailed, well-structured essays on complex topics. CAN write formal letters with appropriate style and register. CAN write a synthesis of information from different sources with reasoned conclusions. |
| **C1** — Advanced | Understand demanding, longer texts with implied meaning and idiomatic expression. Express ideas fluently and spontaneously without visible effort. Produce clear, well-structured, detailed text on complex subjects with controlled use of organisational patterns. Use language flexibly and effectively for academic, professional and social purposes. | CAN understand a wide range of demanding, longer texts and recognise implicit meaning and tone. CAN express ideas fluently, spontaneously and precisely without apparent searching for words. CAN use language flexibly and effectively for academic, professional and social purposes. CAN produce clear, well-structured, detailed text with controlled use of discourse markers. CAN understand and use idiomatic expressions, colloquialisms and humour appropriately. CAN critically evaluate arguments and viewpoints in complex professional or academic contexts. | CAN understand extended speech even when not clearly structured and with implicit meaning. CAN understand any kind of spoken language including fast native-speaker speech. CAN express ideas fluently and spontaneously, rarely appearing to search for words. CAN give a clear, well-structured presentation on a complex topic adapting to audience needs. CAN use language flexibly in complex negotiations and high-stakes professional conversations. CAN engage in extended academic discourse, debate and argumentation with precision and nuance. | CAN understand long, complex factual and literary texts including specialised texts and manuals. CAN understand abstract, structurally complex or highly colloquial literary and non-literary writing. CAN produce well-structured, detailed essays, research papers and professional reports with precision. CAN write clear, smooth-flowing text in an appropriate style with effective logical structure. CAN express complex ideas with nuance, using a wide range of vocabulary and cohesive devices. |

## Notes for product team

| Topic | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Placement test** | Learners should be placed via an in-app adaptive test covering all four skills before their first session |
| **Level progression** | Advancement requires demonstrating competency in at least 80% of Can-Do outcomes across all skill areas |
| **Content mapping** | Each unit should be tagged with the specific Can-Do outcome(s) it addresses to enable progress tracking |
| **C2 scope** | C2 (Mastery) is not in current scope — add when roadmap supports native-speaker-equivalent content |
