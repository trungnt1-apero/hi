> ### Learning tip
> If you speak a language that has borrowed words from English, some English words may look similar to words in your language. This will help you understand words you do not know. For example, the following Malay words are very similar in English:
> - pasport *(passport)*
> - kastam *(customs)*
> - teksi *(taxi)*
> - bas *(bus)*
> - tiket *(ticket)*

> ---

> Changi Airport is located 20 km east of Singapore city centre.
>
> **Public transport into Singapore**
>
> **TRAIN:** The MRT (Mass Rapid Transit) is the quickest way (journey time: approximately 30 minutes) into the city centre. The MRT station is at Terminals 2 and 3. Some trains run between the airport and *Tanah Merah* station only; change there for trains to the city. Others go directly to major stations including *City Hall* and *Raffles Place*. A single journey costs SGD 2.00–3.00. Trains run every 4–8 minutes from 05:31 to 23:18 daily.
>
> **TAXI:** There are always taxis at the airport. The taxi stands are outside the Arrival halls at all terminals. There is a taxi information desk inside the Arrival hall if you need help. The taxi rank is outside Exit A. Estimated fare to the city centre: SGD 20–40.
>
> **BUS:** Public bus service 36 runs between Changi Airport and the city centre (Orchard Road). Buses operate every 10–15 minutes from 06:00 to midnight. The first stop is outside Terminal 2 Arrival hall. The last bus leaves the airport at 00:05. Journey time: approximately 60 minutes. Fare: SGD 2.50.
>
> **SHUTTLE:** Hotel shuttle buses are available for selected hotels. Ask at the Ground Transport Desk in the Arrival hall for schedules and prices.
